# 15 Mentor Guide

This guide helps mentors and coaches use Data and Agents Lab with students over a short preparation window.

## Purpose

The goal is not to lecture students through every file.

The goal is to help students:
- start coding quickly
- build confidence through repetition
- learn how to debug
- use AI as a helper, not autopilot
- explain what they are doing

## Suggested 4-Week Pacing

### Week 1
Focus:
- notebook basics
- Python TODO practice
- using AI well

Recommended notebooks:
- 00
- 01
- 04

What to watch for:
- students running cells out of order
- students changing too much at once
- students asking AI for full answers too early

### Week 2
Focus:
- pandas and data basics
- API/JSON basics
- debugging

Recommended notebooks:
- 02
- 03
- 07

What to watch for:
- students getting lost in DataFrame syntax
- students not inspecting data before acting
- students ignoring error messages

### Week 3
Focus:
- plotting
- classic AI practice
- multi-step data investigation

Recommended notebooks:
- 05
- 08
- 10

What to watch for:
- students making charts without interpreting them
- students writing rules but not testing edge cases
- students jumping to answers without explaining reasoning

### Week 4
Focus:
- agentic AI path
- workflow thinking
- reviewing weak vs strong outputs

Recommended notebooks:
- 06
- 09

What to watch for:
- students writing vague prompts
- students not noticing missing output details
- students treating “sounds good” as “is good”

## Good Mentor Moves

- ask students what they expected before they ran code
- ask them to explain what changed
- encourage one small fix at a time
- redirect them to inspect actual outputs
- let them struggle productively before rescuing too quickly
- ask what AI helped with and what they still had to decide themselves

## Less Helpful Mentor Moves

- taking over the keyboard too quickly
- giving full answers before students try
- treating every error like a crisis
- rewarding speed more than understanding
- letting students submit code they cannot explain

## Good Questions To Ask Students

- What does this variable hold right now?
- What did you expect this code to do?
- What actually happened?
- What is one small thing you can test next?
- What part of the AI answer do you trust? What part do you need to verify?
- How do you know this result is reasonable?

## Signs A Student Is Ready To Move On

A student is probably ready for the next notebook when they can:
- complete the current notebook with limited help
- explain the main idea of the task
- debug at least one small issue independently
- describe how they tested the result
- ask focused questions instead of vague ones

## Final Reminder

The strongest students are not the ones who memorize the most.

They are the ones who:
- read carefully
- test patiently
- debug calmly
- use AI wisely
- explain their work
